## Sample 1 — Clean Reading

### Transcript

> "I've been going through the same routine for months and it's actually helping. Work is predictable. I know what's coming. I know what I'm doing. There's something almost boring about it but I don't want to disturb it. I built this and I want to keep it. I don't know if that's ambition or just relief."

### Geometry Output (illustrative)

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Spread shape | Coherent — all three cards cluster near Stability+ / Continuity+ |
| MatchScore (all cards) | Commit band |

| Position | Card | Orientation | Primary axis |
|---|---|---|---|
| Present | Four of Pentacles | Upright | Stability+ |
| Past | Ten of Pentacles | Upright | Continuity+ |
| Future | Ace of Swords | Upright | Clarity+ |

### Voice

---

These three cards are in the same territory. Stability, continuity, discernment. The reading is not about change — it is about what is being held and whether you can trust what you're seeing.

---

**Past — Ten of Pentacles**

The card shows a family gathered under an archway — the pattern of pentacles overhead, older than any of them.

What you've been in, in the period before this reading, is a pattern that has been accumulating for longer than this particular routine. Ten of Pentacles reads the past as continuity — not just what you've maintained this month, but what has been built across time. The archway in the image is not recent. The pattern of pentacles was already there. You moved under it. That's not nothing.

The months of predictability you described have a history behind them. You didn't start from zero.

*What made it possible to build what you've built? What was already there before you arrived?*

---

**Present — Four of Pentacles**

The card shows a figure with a coin held tight to the chest — city behind, the grip complete.

You said you built this and you want to keep it. Four of Pentacles reads that correctly: what is stable in you right now is a very specific form of holding. Not spreading out. Not letting go. The grip is deliberate. The coin is against the chest, not on the table.

The ambiguity you named — ambition or relief — doesn't need to be resolved here. Both can live in the same grip. The card doesn't distinguish between them because the stability signal is real either way.

What you're in right now is earned, and you know it.

*What does it feel like to let yourself be as settled as you actually are?*

---

**Future — Ace of Swords**

The card shows a single sword held upright, emerging from cloud — the blade clean, a crown balanced at its tip.

What's beginning to emerge from this stable ground is discernment. The Ace of Swords is not a card of uncertainty — it's a card of the honest cut. After months of maintaining, the next signal is assessment: what is this, really? Is it enough, and in what sense?

The "boring" you mentioned might be the beginning of that sword appearing. Not a crisis. Not ambition for its own sake. The edge of the blade asking: what is actually true here?

*What would you see if you looked at this period of your life without needing it to mean something particular?*

---

### Hollow Check

1. **Anchor present?** Yes — each card named its image anchor plainly before drift began.
2. **Could apply to most users?** No — interpretations are grounded in the specific axes (Stability+, Continuity+, Clarity+) and in transcript language ("I built this," "keep it," "boring"). The grip-as-deliberate-holding is Four of Pentacles specific. The archway-as-prior-pattern is Ten of Pentacles specific.
3. **Generic emotional language only?** No — "the grip is against the chest, not on the table" and "the blade asking what is actually true" are card-specific.

**Hollow flag: false**

## Sample 2 — Low-Signal / Exploratory Register

### Transcript

> "I'm not sure what's going on with me lately. I feel kind of scattered. Some good things are happening but I'm not really present for them. I keep thinking about something that happened a few weeks ago but I can't figure out why it keeps coming back. I'm a bit stuck."

### Geometry Output (illustrative)

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Spread shape | Tensioned — Present and Future on opposing poles |
| MatchScore | Present: Exploratory band (0.52). Past: Commit band (0.70). Future: Exploratory band (0.48) |

| Position | Card | Orientation | Primary axis |
|---|---|---|---|
| Past | Six of Swords | Upright | Rupture−/Volatility− |
| Present | Seven of Cups | Upright | Obscurity−/Contraction+ |
| Future | Ace of Wands | Upright | Action+ |

### Voice

---

These three cards are not in the same place. Past and Future are pulling in different directions. The reading holds that tension without resolving it.

This reading carries lower confidence than usual on two of the three cards. What follows offers two ways to hold each of those cards. Neither is asserted as the correct one.

---

**Past — Six of Swords** *(Commit band — standard register)*

The card shows a boat crossing water — choppy on the near side, calm on the far side, the passage not yet complete.

Something happened a few weeks ago. Six of Swords reads what precedes the present moment as a crossing — something you were in the middle of, something being moved through. The swords standing upright in the prow are what you carried with you. They weren't left behind.

The boat is mid-passage. The far side is calm. You are not there yet.

*What did you bring with you when you left that near-side water?*

---

**Present — Seven of Cups** *(Exploratory band — two interpretations)*

The card shows seven cups suspended in cloud, each containing something different — none of them clearly graspable.

This reading is less certain on what signal is dominant in your current state. Here are two ways to hold the Seven of Cups:

*One way:* The cups are interior — the scattered feeling you named is exactly this card. Multiple possibilities, none of them resolved. "Can't figure out why it keeps coming back" is the fog being honest. It hasn't settled yet. That's not a problem. The unformed wants to form; the seven cups want to become one.

*Another way:* The cups are projections. The scattered feeling isn't uncertainty about which direction to go — it's that none of the directions you're seeing in the cloud are real. The card may be asking not "which cup" but "why am I looking at cups instead of what's actually in front of me?"

*Which of these feels closer to what's true — even if neither is exactly right?*

---

**Future — Ace of Wands** *(Exploratory band — two interpretations)*

The card shows a wooden staff held upright, new leaves already growing from the sides — the grip just made.

Here are two ways to hold the Ace of Wands in the Future position:

*One way:* Something small is beginning to move. The leaves are already growing on that wand — the beginning has started before you committed to it. The scattered feeling may be the pre-condition for a first move rather than a wall against it.

*Another way:* The Ace of Wands in Future, when the Present is still foggy, may be premature. Picking up the wand before the seven cups have resolved can produce movement that doesn't go anywhere meaningful. The question might be less "what do I start?" and more "is it time to start?"

*What would it feel like to pick something up — just one thing — and hold it?*

---

### Hollow Check

1. **Anchor present?** Yes — each card named its image anchor first.
2. **Could apply to most users?** The exploratory register is inherently more general by design (low MatchScore means the selection is less specific). The two-interpretation structure acknowledges the ambiguity honestly. Interpretations are grounded in axes (Obscurity−, Contraction+, Action+) and transcript language ("scattered," "can't figure out why," "stuck"). The specific cup imagery ("none clearly graspable," "which cup vs. what's in front of me") is Seven of Cups specific.
3. **Generic only?** No — "the swords standing upright in the prow are what you carried" and "the leaves are already growing before you committed" are card-specific.

**Hollow flag: false** — *Note: Exploratory band readings carry higher hollow risk. Verify the two-interpretation structure is pulling from axes and reversed pole, not from generic emotional vocabulary.*

## Sample 3 — Stalker Card Recurrence

### Context

The user has received Seven of Cups in the Present position in a previous reading (within the current session window). The card has appeared again.

For full stalker card language protocol, see A-8. This sample demonstrates voice integration of that language.

### Transcript

> "Still feeling the same kind of blur. A lot going on but none of it landing. I can't tell if things are improving or if I'm just getting used to feeling unclear. Maybe it's both."

### Geometry Output (illustrative)

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Spread shape | Coherent |
| MatchScore (all cards) | Commit band |

| Position | Card | Orientation | Primary axis |
|---|---|---|---|
| Past | Four of Cups | Upright | Self+/Contraction+ |
| Present | Seven of Cups | Upright (RECURRENCE) | Obscurity−/Contraction+ |
| Future | Ace of Swords | Upright | Clarity+ |

### Voice

---

These three cards are in interior territory — self-observation, interior fog, and then discernment. The thread is the self looking at itself through unclear water.

---

**Past — Four of Cups** *(Commit band)*

The card shows a figure with arms crossed, three cups on the ground — a fourth offered from cloud, unacknowledged.

What has been present in the period before this reading is a quality of inward attention that was not reaching outward. Four of Cups reads the recent past as a kind of sustained watching — the self observing its own states, not doing much with what it saw. The crossed arms are not hostility. They are containment.

The fourth cup — offered and unnoticed — may be the things that have been happening that you weren't fully present for. Not because you didn't care. Because the attention was turned inward.

*What have you been noticing about yourself lately that you haven't said out loud?*

---

**Present — Seven of Cups** *(Commit band — with recurrence note)*

The card shows seven cups suspended in cloud, each containing something different — none of them clearly graspable.

This card is returning. Sometimes that's just chance; sometimes it's a theme you're ready to meet again.

Seven of Cups is showing up in your Present position for the second time. The transcript you shared today is different, but the signal is the same: interior fog, the unformed, what has not yet resolved into knowing. "Can't tell if things are improving or just getting used to feeling unclear" — that's the seven cups describing themselves.

None of the cups in the image are clearly graspable. That's not a failure of the image — it's what the card is. The fog is the state. It hasn't been rushed.

Four of Cups behind it and Ace of Swords ahead suggest: you have been watching, and clarity is coming. But the seven cups are not premature. The fog is doing something.

*What would it mean to trust the fog instead of trying to clear it?*

---

**Future — Ace of Swords** *(Commit band)*

The card shows a single sword held upright, emerging from cloud — the blade clean, a crown balanced at its tip.

What is moving toward you from this interior period is honest assessment. The Ace of Swords is the card after the fog — not clarity for its own sake, but the moment when the blade appears and the honest question becomes possible. What is actually true. Not what you hoped, not what feared, but what is.

The question you asked — "maybe it's both" — is the beginning of the Ace of Swords. Both can be true. The sword wants to weigh them. It doesn't declare a winner.

*When the fog does lift — what is the first thing you want to see clearly?*

---

### Hollow Check

1. **Anchor present?** Yes — each card named its anchor before drift began.
2. **Could apply to most users?** The recurrence note is specific to this session's history. The Four of Cups / Seven of Cups / Ace of Swords spread is grounded in the specific axes (Self+/Obscurity−/Clarity+) and in transcript language ("blur," "none of it landing," "can't tell if improving"). The recurrence of Seven of Cups makes this reading unusually specific to this user's session.
3. **Generic only?** No — "the fourth cup offered and unnoticed" is Four of Cups specific. "None of the cups in the image are clearly graspable — that's what the card is" is Seven of Cups specific. "The blade appears and the honest question becomes possible" is Ace of Swords specific.

**Hollow flag: false**
