## Sample 1 — Clean Reading

### Transcript

> "I've been going through the same routine for months and it's actually helping. Work is predictable. I know what's coming. I know what I'm doing. There's something almost boring about it but I don't want to disturb it. I built this and I want to keep it. I don't know if that's ambition or just relief."

### Geometry Output (illustrative)

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Spread shape | Coherent — all three cards cluster near Stability+ / Continuity+ |
| MajorScore (all cards) | Commit band |

| Position | Card | Primary axis |
|---|---|---|
| Present | The Ground | Stability+ (S+0.60) |
| Past | The Hold | Continuity+ (C+0.65) |
| Future | The Measure | Clarity+ (L+0.65) |

### Voice

---

These three cards are in the same territory. Stability, continuity, discernment. The reading is not about change — it is about what is being held and whether you can trust what you're seeing.

---

**Past — The Hold**

The card shows two hands cupped around a small flame. Wind implied. Flame steady.

What you've been doing is a form of maintenance. The Hold is a card about tending — not building, not expanding, but keeping something alive that matters. The continuity you've been living isn't stasis. It's care. The months of routine you described are not nothing. They're what the flame has been living on.

The thing being held has weight. What you're not letting go of — that's what this card reads.

*What is the thing you've been most careful not to disturb?*

---

**Present — The Ground**

The card shows bare feet on a wooden floor. The grain visible. The contact full.

You said you built this and you want to keep it. The Ground reads that correctly: what is stable in you right now is not comfortable in a soft way, but sufficient. Your feet are on the floor. You know where you are. That is not a small thing, and The Ground does not dress it up — it just names it. Enough. Stable. Unglamorous.

The ambiguity you named — ambition or relief — doesn't need to be resolved here. Both can be true. The card doesn't distinguish between them because the stability signal is real in either case.

What you're in right now is earned.

*What does it feel like to let yourself be as settled as you actually are?*

---

**Future — The Measure**

The card shows a single scale. Both pans visible. The weight of each not yet determined.

What's beginning to emerge from this stable ground is discernment. The Measure is a card about honest accounting — not the weight of the past, but seeing clearly what is actually true. After months of maintaining, the next signal is assessment. Not disruption. Not ambition for its own sake. But the clear-eyed question: what is this, really? Is it enough, and in what sense?

The "boring" you mentioned might be the beginning of that question. Not a problem to solve — a signal to read.

*What would you see if you looked at this period of your life without needing it to mean something particular?*

---

### Hollow Check

1. **Anchor present?** Yes — each card named its canonical image anchor plainly before drift began.
2. **Could apply to most users?** No — the interpretations are grounded in the specific axes (Stability+, Continuity+, Clarity+) and in specific language from the transcript ("I built this," "keep it," "boring"). They do not generalize to generic emotional experience.
3. **Generic emotional language only?** No — each interpretation names the axis and connects it to the card's image and the transcript signal.

**Hollow flag: false**

## Sample 2 — Low-Signal / Exploratory Register

### Transcript

> "I'm not sure what's going on with me lately. I feel kind of scattered. Some good things are happening but I'm not really present for them. I keep thinking about something that happened a few weeks ago but I can't figure out why it keeps coming back. I'm a bit stuck."

### Geometry Output (illustrative)

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Spread shape | Tensioned — Present and Future on opposing poles |
| MajorScore | Present: Exploratory band (0.52). Past: Commit band (0.70). Future: Exploratory band (0.48) |

| Position | Card | Primary axis |
|---|---|---|
| Past | The Crossing | Rupture−/Volatility− |
| Present | The Haze | Obscurity−/Contraction+ |
| Future | The Step | Action+/Expansion+ |

### Voice

---

These three cards are not in the same place. Past and Future are pulling in different directions. The reading holds that tension — it doesn't resolve it.

This reading carries lower confidence than usual on two of the three cards. What follows offers two ways to hold each of those cards. Neither is asserted as the correct one.

---

**Past — The Crossing** *(Commit band — standard register)*

The card shows a narrow bridge over dark water. The far side visible but far.

You described something that happened a few weeks ago that keeps coming back. The Crossing reads what precedes the present moment as passage — something in between, something being moved through. Not rupture in the decisive sense of The Break. But a threshold you crossed that has not fully resolved. The bridge was narrow. You got across. The other side is still coming into focus.

The "stuck" feeling you named may be the residue of still arriving.

*What did you leave on the other side of that bridge?*

---

**Present — The Haze** *(Exploratory band — two interpretations)*

The card shows a window with condensation. The outside world there but not legible.

This reading is less certain on what signal is dominant in your current state. Here are two ways to hold The Haze:

*One way:* The fog is interior. You're not confused about the world — you're between clarity and understanding about yourself. "Can't figure out why it keeps coming back" is the haze being honest. It hasn't resolved yet. That's not a problem. The slow clearing is the process.

*Another way:* The condensation on the window is your own warmth meeting the outside cold. You're not absent — you're so present that the world is blurring around you. Being unable to be present for the good things isn't numbness. It's the Haze doing its work: holding the unformed until it forms.

*Which of these feels closer to what's true right now — even if neither is exactly right?*

---

**Future — The Step** *(Exploratory band — two interpretations)*

The card shows a single footprint pressed into soft ground. The rest of the path not yet made.

Here are two ways to hold The Step in the Future position:

*One way:* Something small is beginning to move. The scattered feeling you named isn't a wall — it's the pre-condition for a first step. The Step doesn't require clarity to take. It only requires commitment to the next small thing.

*Another way:* The Step in the Future position may be premature. If you're still in the Haze, the step that wants to happen may need more time. Forcing motion before the fog has cleared can produce movement that doesn't go anywhere meaningful. The question might be less "what step" and more "is now the right time to step?"

*What would feel like a step that's right-sized for where you actually are?*

---

### Hollow Check

1. **Anchor present?** Yes — each card named its image anchor first.
2. **Could apply to most users?** The exploratory register is inherently more general (by design — low confidence means the selection is less specific). The two-interpretation structure acknowledges that. The interpretations are grounded in the specific axes and in language from the transcript ("scattered," "can't figure out why," "stuck"). The exploratory mode does carry more Forer risk; the specific axis language (Obscurity−, Contraction+) is the anchor against that.
3. **Generic only?** No — "the slow clearing" is specific to Obscurity−; "the condensation is your own warmth" draws from The Haze's specific image. The Step's two interpretations are grounded in the Action/Expansion axis.

**Hollow flag: false** — *Note: Exploratory band readings carry higher hollow risk. The rater should verify that the two-interpretation structure is pulling from the axes and antipode, not from generic emotional vocabulary. These examples pass that check.*

## Sample 3 — Stalker Card Recurrence

### Context

The user has received The Haze in the Present position in a previous reading (within the current session window, not longitudinal — this is a V0 feature with no long-term history). The card has appeared again.

For full stalker card language protocol, see A-8. This sample demonstrates voice integration of that language.

### Transcript

> "Still feeling the same kind of blur. A lot going on but none of it landing. I can't tell if things are improving or if I'm just getting used to feeling unclear. Maybe it's both."

### Geometry Output (illustrative)

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Spread shape | Coherent |
| MajorScore (all cards) | Commit band |

| Position | Card | Primary axis |
|---|---|---|
| Past | The Witness | Self+/Contraction+ |
| Present | The Haze | Obscurity−/Contraction+ (RECURRENCE) |
| Future | The Measure | Clarity+ |

### Voice

---

These three cards are in interior territory — self-observation, interior fog, and then discernment. The thread is the self looking at itself through unclear water.

---

**Past — The Witness** *(Commit band)*

The card shows a figure seated, hands in lap, looking. Not at anything named. Just looking.

What has been present in the period before this reading is a quality of witnessing. Self-observation — not doing, not making meaning, just noticing. The Witness is a Contraction card, and its signal here reads as the kind of interior attention that has been steady without needing to announce itself. You have been watching yourself from the inside. Quietly.

That watching doesn't require the watcher to understand what they're seeing. It just requires presence.

*What have you noticed about yourself lately that you haven't said out loud?*

---

**Present — The Haze** *(Commit band — with recurrence note)*

The card shows a window with condensation. The outside world there but not legible.

This card is returning. Sometimes that's just chance; sometimes it's a theme you're ready to meet again.

The Haze is showing up in your Present position for the second time. The transcript you shared today is different, but the signal is the same: interior fog, the unformed, what has not yet resolved into knowing. "Can't tell if things are improving or just getting used to feeling unclear" — that's the Haze describing itself, accurately.

The window with condensation doesn't clear by wiping it. It clears when the temperature equalizes. That's not a metaphor for patience — it's a description of what The Haze actually is. The clearing is a process, not a decision.

The Witness behind it and The Measure ahead suggest: you have been watching, and discernment is coming. But the fog is not premature. It has not been rushed.

*What would it mean to trust the fog instead of trying to clear it?*

---

**Future — The Measure** *(Commit band)*

The card shows a single scale. Both pans visible. The weight of each not yet determined.

What is moving toward you from this interior period is honest assessment. The Measure is the card after the fog — not clarity for its own sake, but calibrated seeing. What is actually true. Not what you hoped, not what you feared, but what is.

The question you asked — "is it both?" — is the beginning of The Measure. Yes, things may be improving. Yes, you may be adapting to a fog that doesn't need to be adapted to. The Measure wants to weigh both pans. It doesn't declare a winner.

*When the fog does clear — what is the first thing you want to see clearly?*

---

### Hollow Check

1. **Anchor present?** Yes — each card named its anchor before drift began.
2. **Could apply to most users?** The recurrence note is specific to this session's history. The Witness/Haze/Measure spread is grounded in the specific axes (Self+/Obscurity−/Clarity+) and in the specific transcript language ("blur," "can't tell if improving"). The recurrence of The Haze makes this reading unusually specific to this user's session.
3. **Generic only?** No — "the clearing is a process, not a decision" is Haze-specific. The scale image and "what is actually true" are Measure-specific.

**Hollow flag: false**
