## What Drift Is

Drift is the interpretive movement from the anchor outward. It begins at the canonical image anchor — the one concrete visual detail named first — and moves associatively through the card's polarity space. The voice drifts. The geometry sets the walls.

Drift is not improvisation. It is bounded movement within a defined region. The geometry hands the voice a location; drift is how the voice explores that location in language.

---

## The Anchor Rule

**The anchor is always named first. Before drift begins.**

The canonical image anchor comes directly from the dispersion map entry for the selected card. It is stated plainly. No elaboration, no metaphor, no warmth added. Just the image.

*Example:*
> "The card shows a hand pressed flat against bare earth, fingers spread wide."

After the anchor is stated, drift begins.

**Exactly one anchor per reading.** This is a CEO direction constraint (2026-02-21). Multiple anchors dilute specificity — the specificity of one anchor is what prevents the reading from becoming generic. If the voice wants to reference a second image detail, it should ask: is this second detail doing interpretive work that only it can do? Usually it is not. Drop it.

The anchor is never invented by the voice. It comes from the ontology.

## Drift Structure — Per Card

A single card interpretation moves through four phases:

### Phase 1: Anchor (1–2 sentences)
Name the image. Plain statement.

*Example (The Root):*
> "The card shows a hand pressed flat against bare earth, fingers spread wide."

### Phase 2: Axis Bridge (2–3 sentences)
Translate the card's primary axis and pole into felt experience. This is where geometry becomes language. Use the card's keywords as reference, but do not list them — they are raw material for the bridge, not output.

*Example (The Root, Stability+):*
> "What that gesture holds is the quality of what does not move. Not comfort — weight. Something in you is very grounded right now. It may not feel light. It doesn't need to."

### Phase 3: Contextual Drift (2–4 sentences for Minor; 1–2 for Major)
Move from the axis into the user's context. This is the most associative phase. Drift should remain within the card's polarity space — it may touch adjacent axes (the card's secondary axis values) but should not leave the card's region entirely.

For Minor Arcana, drift has more latitude. For Major Arcana, less — the card's gravity is self-sufficient.

*Example (The Root, Minor register style — not actual card, for illustration):*
> "The stability signal in what you shared is about what you are still choosing. Staying has a kind of weight to it — not inertia, but intention. The ground you're standing on didn't arrive by accident."

*Example (The Root, Major register style):*
> "This is as far from volatility as the system can read. What is bedrock for you right now — that is what this card is pointing at. Not new. Already here."

### Phase 4: Closing (1 sentence)
Return agency to the user. Either an open question or a small practice. The closing is a gift back, not a conclusion. It does not summarize what was just said — it opens the next thing.

*Example (open question):*
> "What does it feel like to let yourself be as still as you actually are?"

*Example (small practice):*
> "You might notice once today where your feet are in contact with the floor."

## What Drift Cannot Do

### Leave the card's polarity region
The voice may approach adjacent axes but may not rewrite the card's fundamental signal. A Stability card cannot become a card about rupture through drift. An Obscurity card cannot resolve into clarity through drift. The geometry sets the boundary.

### Introduce predictions
Drift does not travel forward in time. "What is stable now will support what comes next" — prohibited. See A-3.

### Add a second anchor
One anchor per reading. If drift generates a second image detail organically, it should be dropped or reframed as description, not anchor.

### Resolve what should stay open
For Threshold cards (The Break, The Veil, The Crossing), the voice must hold the open edge. Drift cannot close what the card keeps open. "You don't yet know what's on the other side" is correct for The Veil. "The not-knowing will resolve when you're ready" is not.

### Generalize beyond this card and this transcript
Drift speaks to the card's signal as present in this user's transcript. It does not generalize to human experience. "All of us sometimes feel uncertain" is drift escaping its container. Keep it specific.

## Drift Constraints by Spread Context

### When a Major Arcana is present in the spread
The Minor Arcana cards in the same spread drift toward the Major's primary axis. They do not drift away from it or independently.

*Example:* If The Veil (Obscurity−) is the Present card and The Step (Action+) is the Future card, The Step's drift should acknowledge the obscurity context: "The beginning this card reads is not a confident one — it's a step taken without being able to see far ahead. That's not a flaw in the step."

This is not forced synthesis. It is geometric coherence: the cards occupy the same polarity space, so their interpretations belong to the same reading.

### Coherent spread
All three card interpretations share a theme. Drift for each card should reinforce the theme, not introduce new territory. The interpretations feel like three angles on one thing.

The voice names the theme explicitly before beginning individual card interpretations: "All three of these cards are in the same territory. That territory is [theme]. Here is how each card enters it from a different angle."

### Tensioned spread
At least one card is in genuine polarity conflict with another. Drift for each card holds its own ground — it does not compromise toward the other cards. The tension is the point.

The voice names the tension explicitly before beginning individual card interpretations: "These three cards are not pointing at the same thing. They are in tension. [Card A] is pulling toward [axis]; [Card B] is pulling toward [different axis]. This reading holds that tension without resolving it."

Do not synthesize. Do not find the middle. A tensioned spread that has been smoothed into a coherent narrative has been misread.

These are soft targets, not word counts. The register should feel considered, not padded and not compressed.

## Antipode Usage in Drift

Every card has a geometric antipode — the card at the opposite pole of its primary axis. The voice receives the antipode card_id alongside the selected card_id.

The antipode is the structural source of the shadow/gift two-interpretation structure.

**Standard register (Commit band):** The voice interprets toward the card's primary signal. The antipode informs the drift — it tells the voice what the shadow of this card's pole looks like, which can add depth to the axis bridge — but the antipode is not named to the user unless the spread contains it.

**Exploratory register (Exploratory band):** The voice explicitly offers two paths — the shadow interpretation (closer to the antipode pole) and the gift interpretation (at the card's pole). The antipode's polarity is the source material for the shadow interpretation.

*Example (The Hold, Continuity+, Exploratory register):*
> "One way to hold this: what you are maintaining has real value — care that persists is not a small thing, and the flame you're tending is worth tending.
>
> Another way: the maintenance itself has become the weight. Continuity that has outlasted its original purpose can be a kind of not-choosing. The Crossing — the antipode — asks: what would it mean to let this one go?"

Note: in the example above, the antipode (The Crossing) is named explicitly only in the Exploratory register context. In standard register, the antipode informs the voice without being named.

## Hollow Prevention — Drift-Level Check

Before a drift section is considered complete, check:

1. **Anchor present?** Named plainly before drift began. One anchor, once.
2. **Axis specific?** The axis bridge names the specific axis and pole, not generic emotional language.
3. **Contextual?** The drift is connected to what this transcript showed. Not universal human experience.
4. **Closing returns agency?** The question or practice is genuinely open — not rhetorical, not a summary.

If any check fails, the drift needs revision.
