## The Question

The three-axis reduction exercise (conducted during long-term vision research) found that if forced to keep only three of six axes, the three I would keep are:

1. **Stability ↔ Volatility** (S)
2. **Contraction ↔ Expansion** (X)
3. **Clarity ↔ Obscurity** (L)

These three axes describe a person's internal state: how grounded they feel, how open or closed they are, and how clearly they can see. The three dropped axes — Continuity/Rupture, Self/Other, Reception/Action — are about relationship to the world, to others, and to events. The reduction exercise revealed that the system's deepest logic is interior-first.

The question asked: **Is that the right worldview for V0?**

---

## Answer: Confirmed, with a clarification

**Yes — V0 is primarily about interiority.** This is not an accident of the reduction exercise; it reflects a deliberate design position. The product is a tool for a person to understand their own inner life, not a tool for predicting events or navigating external circumstances.

**The clarification:** The three dropped axes are not peripheral. They are the context that makes interiority legible. A person's internal state of stability or volatility only means something in relation to what is happening around them (Continuity/Rupture), who else is involved (Self/Other), and what they are doing or being called to do (Reception/Action). Dropping these axes in the reduction exercise does not mean they are unimportant — it means they are *less load-bearing for the core selection logic*.

The geometry holds all six axes. The interiority-first worldview is about where interpretive weight is concentrated, not about which axes exist.

---

## Implications for the Ontology

**Card distribution reflects interiority-first:**
- Interior bucket: 4 cards (same as Foundation and Motion)
- Relation bucket: 3 cards (smaller by design)
- This is correct for V0. Post-V0 expansion should grow the Relation bucket first.

**Weight distribution reflects interiority-first:**
- The similarity weighting table (I-5) gives S and X the highest base weights (0.22 and 0.20)
- O (Self/Other) and A (Reception/Action) have the lowest base weights (0.14 and 0.10)
- This is correct. It means transcripts that don't have strong relational or action signals still produce meaningful card selections.

**Major Arcana placement reflects interiority-first:**
- Of the six Majors, two are interior-dominant (The Mirror: Contraction/Self; The Veil: Obscurity)
- Two are threshold-dominant (The Root: Stability; The Break: Rupture)
- One is motion-dominant (The Current: Action)
- One is relational (The Weave: Other/Expansion)
- The interior axis is the only one with two Majors in its core register. This is intentional.

---

## Implications for the Voice

*Flagged for Amara:*

The interiority-first worldview means that even when a Relation card is selected (The Bridge, The Echo, The Weave), the voice should interpret it from the inside out. "Here is what this connection means *to you*" — not "here is what the other person is like." The system reads the person, not the situation.

This is a significant voice constraint: relational cards do not get interpreted as analysis of other people. They are read as the user's experience of being in relation.

---

## What This Does Not Foreclose

- **Post-V0 expansion** can shift the balance. If evidence from live testing shows users consistently bring relational transcripts and find the readings insufficient, the Relation bucket can grow and the O/A weights can be revised.
- **Expanded readings** (post-V0) might have a different orientation — more situational, more outward-facing, a different frame than P/P/F. The interiority-first constraint applies to V0's daily reading format, not to the system as a whole.
- **The relational Majors (The Weave) and threshold Majors (The Break)** can still produce powerful readings. The interiority-first posture shapes *how* they are interpreted, not whether they appear.
