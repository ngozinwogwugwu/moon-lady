## Upright / Reversed Convention

Replaces the antipode pairing system. Each card in this deck is its own opposite when reversed.

**Upright:** The card's primary pole. Stage A selects this when the transcript maps positively to the card's primary axis.

**Reversed:** The card's opposite pole. Stage A selects this when the transcript maps to the inverse of the card's primary axis but the card is still the best geometric match. Stage A outputs `card_orientation: "upright" | "reversed"` alongside the card_id.

**Voice usage:** The orientation is an internal system concept — never surfaced to the user directly. In the Commit register, the voice interprets toward the orientation silently. In the Exploratory register, the voice offers both the upright interpretation and the reversed interpretation as "one way to hold this / another way." The word "reversed" is never used with the user.

**Orientation decision rule (Stage A):** For a given card, if the highest-contributing axis in the transcript vector aligns with the card's primary pole (same sign), the card is upright. If it aligns with the opposite sign but the card still scores highest in similarity, the card is reversed. Ties broken deterministically by card-ID ordering.

## Spread Shape Definition

As agreed in cross-session (I-1):

**Coherent spread:** All three selected cards have at least two axes within ±0.25 of each other. The spread points at one region of polarity space from different angles.

**Tensioned spread:** At least one card is more than 0.60 apart from another card on a single axis. The spread contains genuine polarity conflict.

Stage A outputs `spread_shape: "coherent"` or `spread_shape: "tensioned"` alongside the card selections. Amara's register rules use this signal.

## Notes for Voice Spec

*Flagged for Amara:*

- **Image anchors** reference Rider-Waite imagery. Cards have established visual language — anchors draw on specific, recognizable elements of each card's illustration.
- **Upright/reversed** orientation feeds the two-interpretation structure. Reversed interpretation is derived from the card's own reversed pole, not a separate card.
- **Arcana type** should modulate register: Major → less drift, more weight, more silence at close. Minor → more drift latitude.
- **The High Priestess** and **The Moon** have strong Obscurity signals. The voice must not attempt to resolve this — both cards require sitting with what cannot be seen or known.
- **The High Priestess / The Lovers** tension: when both appear in the same spread, the tension between deep inwardness (High Priestess) and full relational expansion (The Lovers) is the reading's structural core. Name it, hold it, do not resolve it.
