# One-Page Vision

- Tarot Company builds a ritual-grade tarot reading app grounded in symbolic depth, not prediction.
- The product serves users seeking reflection, not forecasting — every reading is an invitation, not a verdict.
- Stage A (selector) surfaces cards using a principled algorithm that respects arcana rarity and spread constraints.
- Stage B (interpreter) generates reading text with no access to user session data — it sees only the drawn cards.
- Major Arcana cards appear rarely and carry elevated weight; their presence signals threshold moments.
- Symbol sets are culturally grounded, carefully sourced, and treated as intellectual assets.
- Rituals (lunar events, equinoxes, solstices) will shape the experience over time; MVP scopes to core spread logic only.
- No deterministic predictions. No therapeutic claims. No medical, legal, or financial guidance — ever.
- Resonance, not virality, is the retention metric. Users return because readings feel true, not because of gamification.
- The blind interpreter design (Stage B never sees the user) is a safety and privacy commitment, not just an architecture choice.
- All content decisions are owned by Ngozi; all contributors operate within a documented ownership model.
- The MVP ships documentation-first: specs and contracts precede implementation.

## Appendix: Foundational Intent

This product is motivated by a desire for structured reflection in a secular world.

It aims to create:

- Ritual without doctrine
- Meaning without metaphysical claims
- Attention without analysis

The system must feel intentional, scarce, and symbolically coherent.

Long-term ambition: sustainable, independent ritual product.
